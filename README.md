# Rosendahl Police Misconduct Dataset

> The world's most comprehensive open dataset documenting police misconduct — built for researchers, journalists, legal advocates, and the public record.

---

## What This Is

This repository is a structured, evidence-based dataset cataloging documented incidents of police misconduct. Every case is preserved with primary source material: video footage (extracted as frames), written documentation, and contextual metadata.

This is not commentary. This is a record.

The dataset is maintained under the **Rosendahl** project and follows a strict case-numbering system to ensure traceability, reproducibility, and long-term archival integrity.

---

## Why It Exists

Accountability requires evidence. Evidence requires preservation. Preservation requires organization.

Police misconduct is systematically underreported, undercharged, and underdocumented. Footage disappears. Records are sealed. Witnesses are silenced. This dataset exists to counter that — to create a permanent, structured, and searchable archive that cannot be quietly buried.

Every case here represents a real incident. Every frame extracted, every document filed, every note written is part of a deliberate effort to ensure these events are not forgotten, not minimized, and not erased.

---

## Dataset Structure

Each case follows a consistent directory structure:

```
Rosendahl Case #[N] - [Description]/
  [Incident Name]/
    Video Assault/          # Extracted video frames (JPG)
    CREDITS.txt             # Source attribution and fair use notice
    notes.md                # Case notes and context
```

Cases are numbered sequentially. The numbering is permanent — cases are never renumbered or removed.

## Data Types

Each case may contain one or more of the following:

- **Video frames** — extracted from source footage at regular intervals, preserved as JPG images
- **Case notes** — written documentation of the incident, context, and known outcomes
- **Source credits** — attribution for original footage, including fair use declarations
- **Supporting documents** — PDFs, correspondence, legal filings, or other primary sources

---

## Legal & Ethical Framework

All material in this dataset is collected and preserved under the following principles:

**Fair Use (17 U.S.C. § 107)** — Video frames and excerpts are used for research, documentation, criticism, and public interest journalism. Use is transformative and non-commercial.

**Public Interest** — Documenting police conduct is a matter of legitimate public concern. The preservation of evidence of potential misconduct serves accountability, not prurience.

**No Fabrication** — Nothing in this dataset is fabricated, altered to misrepresent events, or presented out of context to deceive. Source attribution is provided for all material.

**Privacy** — Where possible, the identities of victims are handled with care. The focus is on documenting the conduct of public officials acting in their official capacity.

---

## Intended Use

This dataset is intended for:

- Academic researchers studying police use of force, civil rights, and institutional accountability
- Journalists and investigative reporters
- Legal advocates and civil rights organizations
- Policy analysts and reform advocates
- The general public exercising their right to know

It is **not** intended for harassment, doxxing, or any use that targets individuals outside the context of their official conduct.

## Contributing

If you have documented evidence of police misconduct that meets the standards of this dataset, contributions are welcome. All submissions must include:

1. A verifiable primary source (video, official document, or credible news report)
2. Date, location, and a factual description of the incident
3. Source attribution
