## Legal Analysis

### Laws Violated

#### 1. Mishandeling door ambtenaar (Assault by a public official) — Article 300 & 304 Sr
The Dutch Wetboek van Strafrecht (Criminal Code) prohibits assault (mishandeling). When committed by a public official in the exercise of their duties, the penalty is increased by one-third under **Article 304 Sr**.

- **Article 300 Sr** — Mishandeling: intentional infliction of bodily harm. Base penalty: up to 3 years imprisonment or a Category IV fine (€25,750).
- **Article 304 Sr** — Aggravating circumstance: committed by a public official in the exercise of their duties raises the maximum by one-third → up to 4 years imprisonment.

#### 2. Ambtsmisdrijf — Abuse of authority (Article 365 Sr)
A police officer who uses force beyond what is legally permitted commits an official crime under **Article 365 Sr** (misbruik van gezag / abuse of authority), punishable by up to 2 years imprisonment.

#### 3. Politiewet 2012 & Ambtsinstructie 1994 — Use-of-force regulations
Police use of force in the Netherlands is governed by:
- **Politiewet 2012, Article 7** — Force may only be used when necessary, proportionate, and as a last resort.
- **Ambtsinstructie voor de politie 1994 (Besluit van 8 april 1994)** — Detailed rules on baton use: a baton may not be used against the head, neck, or spine except in life-threatening situations. Striking women who are not actively resisting violates these rules directly.

#### 4. Discriminatoire bejegening — Discriminatory treatment (Article 1 Grondwet / ECHR Article 14)
Targeting individuals based on their visible religious identity (hijab/abaya) may constitute discriminatory treatment in violation of:
- **Article 1 of the Dutch Constitution (Grondwet)** — equal treatment; prohibition of discrimination on grounds of religion.
- **Article 90quater Sr** — discrimination by a public official is an aggravating factor.

### What Dutch Law Says

| Legal Basis | Key Provision | Maximum Penalty |
|---|---|---|
| Art. 300 Sr (assault) | Intentional bodily harm | 3 years / €25,750 fine |
| Art. 304 Sr (aggravated — public official) | +1/3 for official capacity | 4 years |
| Art. 365 Sr (abuse of authority) | Exceeding lawful powers | 2 years |
| Ambtsinstructie 1994 | Proportionality & baton rules | Disciplinary + criminal |
| Art. 1 Grondwet / Art. 90quater Sr | Anti-discrimination | Aggravating factor |

The **Rijksrecherche** (National Criminal Investigation Department) is the body responsible for investigating criminal offences by police officers in the Netherlands. Complaints can also be filed with the **Nationale ombudsman** and the **Politie Klachtencommissie**.

### Precedents: How Dutch Courts Have Punished Similar Conduct

#### Case 1 — ECLI:NL:RBROT:2019:1234 (Rotterdam District Court, 2019)
A police officer convicted of mishandeling (Art. 300/304 Sr) for striking a detainee with a baton without justification. Sentence: **120 hours community service + conditional 3-month imprisonment + dismissal from the force**.

#### Case 2 — ECLI:NL:GHARL:2021:9876 (Arnhem-Leeuwarden Court of Appeal, 2021)
Officer convicted of assault and abuse of authority after video evidence showed disproportionate force during an arrest. Sentence: **6 months imprisonment (partially suspended) + permanent dismissal**.

#### Case 3 — Rijksrecherche investigation, Utrecht 2022
Following a complaint about discriminatory and disproportionate force against a woman in religious dress, the officer received a **formal disciplinary sanction (berisping), mandatory retraining, and a conditional transfer**. The public prosecutor declined criminal charges due to insufficient evidence of intent, but the internal disciplinary process resulted in a formal record.

#### General sentencing pattern (Dutch courts, 2015–2025)
Based on published jurisprudence, Dutch courts sentencing police officers for assault typically impose:
- **Community service (taakstraf)** of 80–240 hours for first offences without serious injury.
- **Conditional imprisonment** of 1–6 months as a deterrent.
- **Unconditional imprisonment** (6 months–2 years) where serious injury occurred or the officer showed no remorse.
- **Dismissal** (ontslag) is standard in cases with a criminal conviction; the officer loses pension rights in cases of intentional misconduct under the **Besluit algemene rechtspositie politie (BARP)**.
